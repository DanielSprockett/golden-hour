# Golden Hour 🌅

A little personal command center for messy, overlapping projects — built for the specific chaos of running fifteen or twenty things at once and constantly losing track of what's actually free to work on.

It's one HTML file. No sign-up, no backend, no subscription, nothing to install. You open it in a browser and it just works.

## What it actually does

- **Projects full of tasks.** Each project (Research / Grants / Writing / Personal / Creative) holds its own list of tasks, and tasks get their own type too — Coding, Lab, Analysis, Writing, Follow-Up, Create.
- **Dependencies that do something.** Mark a task as depending on another one — even a task sitting in a totally different project — and it stays blocked until that other task gets done. You don't have to keep that chain in your head, the app does.
- **A "Work on now" strip.** Always sitting at the top: everything that's actually unblocked right now, sorted with the most urgent stuff first. No more staring at a giant list wondering where to even start.
- **Due dates**, with overdue and due-soon flagged automatically so nothing quietly slips.
- **Priority ranking** for both projects and tasks. Give something a lower number and it floats to the top.
- **Archive + "show completed."** Finished work disappears from your day-to-day view so it doesn't pile up forever, but it's never actually deleted — it's still fully searchable whenever you want it back.
- **Doc links.** Drop a URL in (Google Doc, Notion, wherever) and it shows up as a one-click link right on the project or task.
- **Your data stays on your device.** Everything is saved in your browser's local storage. It never gets sent anywhere, including to wherever this repo happens to be hosted.

## Using it

Open `index.html` in literally any browser — phone or computer, doesn't matter. Add a project, throw some tasks in, start crossing things off.

If you want it to feel more like an app on your phone, most mobile browsers let you "Add to Home Screen" (or "Open as Web App") for a page like this, which gives you a real tappable icon that opens full-screen, no browser bar.

## A quick heads-up about your data

Since everything lives in your browser's local storage, your data is tied to *that specific browser on that specific device*. It won't magically follow you to your laptop or a different phone. There's a Backup button inside the app that copies your data out as plain text, which you can paste back in somewhere else if you ever need to move it.
